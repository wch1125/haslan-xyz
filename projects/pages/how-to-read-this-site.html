How to Read This Site

December 2025

What This Is

This is a public research notebook. It contains essays, sketches, definitions, and speculative fiction exploring recurring themes: consciousness and awareness, institutional power and secrecy, technology and access, narrative control and epistemic freedom.

The work is pattern-oriented rather than ideology-driven. I'm interested in structural dynamics—how intermediation works, how awareness relates to responsibility, how institutions behave under asymmetric information—not in arriving at "the right answer" or recruiting believers.

Think of it as someone trying to stay honest while thinking big, in public.

What This Is Not

  • Not a claim to hidden knowledge. The speculative pieces (like The Watcher Hypothesis) are clearly labeled as fiction. They're thought experiments, not disclosures.
  • Not a solved system. The definitions and cross-references create internal coherence, but coherence is not the same as truth. I could be wrong about all of this.
  • Not recruitment. I'm not trying to build a following or persuade you of a worldview. If these ideas are useful, use them. If not, move on.
  • Not academic work. Some essays invoke research traditions without formal citation. Where I've added "Sources Worth Reading" sections, treat them as starting points, not comprehensive literature reviews.

Content Types

The site uses badges to signal what kind of piece you're reading:

  • Essay: A structured argument. Makes claims, provides reasoning, invites disagreement.
  • Sketch: Exploratory, not concluding. Speculative. Trying on ideas rather than defending them.
  • Fiction: Narrative scaffolding for ideas. Not claims about reality.
  • Definition: A working term used across the site. Not an attempt to replace existing academic language—a translation layer for internal consistency.

Epistemic Guardrails

Each essay includes (or will include) collapsible sections stating:

  • "What this essay is NOT claiming" — explicit disclaimers about scope and intent.
  • "What would change my mind" — falsifiability conditions. What evidence would update the model.

These exist because internal coherence can be a trap. A framework that explains everything explains nothing. The guardrails are there to keep the ideas honest—and to signal that I'm aware of the risks of building private ontologies.

The Definitions Page

The Definitions page is a personal glossary—a backbone for the site's internal vocabulary. It exists because certain concepts recur across essays and need consistent meaning.

It is not an attempt to invent jargon or escape into private language. Many terms have external analogs:

  • Algorithmic Mediation → recommender systems, ranking, framing effects
  • Epistemic Commons → shared baseline facts, public sphere, common knowledge
  • Custodial Secrecy → institutional opacity, classification regimes, information asymmetry

The definitions are translations, not inventions. Use them as a convenience, not a requirement.

How to Engage

Skeptically. The best engagement is critical engagement. If something seems wrong, it might be wrong. The guardrails exist to make disagreement easier, not to prevent it.

Selectively. Not everything here will be relevant to you. Follow what's useful; ignore what isn't.

Generously. This is thinking-in-progress, not a finished product. Some pieces are more polished than others. Some will be revised or abandoned.

About "H. Aslan"

A pseudonym. "Aslan" is Turkish for "lion"—and yes, the Narnia reference is intentional. The tagline "Not a tame lion" comes from C.S. Lewis's description of Aslan: safe, but not tame.

The pseudonym exists not to hide but to separate. This work stands on its own or not at all. The ideas don't need biographical authority; they need to be evaluated on their merits.

Questions or Feedback

If something here is genuinely wrong, I'd like to know. If something resonates, that's useful information too. The contact page exists for a reason.
